Camdyn's Book Reviews

Hello, my name is Camdyn and on this page I will be reviewing some books that I have read.

Sunrise on the Reaping

The Hunger Games prequel, "Sunrise on the Reaping" by Suzanne Collins, tells the backstory of the beloved Haymitch Abernathy, Katniss and Peeta's mentor. It is a dystopian novel and the second prequel to the original Hunger Games trilogy. It is a grief-filled, traumatic story that connects all the previous books together. Haymitch's story will fly by when reading, but will stick with you afterwards. Get ready for a good cry. If you enjoy the Hunger Games, "Sunrise on the Reaping" is a must-read.

We Were Liars

A young-adult novel by E. Lockhart, "We Were Liars" is a compelling story that explores the idea of the consequences of one's mistakes. It tells the story of Cadence Sinclair, a seventeen year old girl and the eldest grandchild of the extremely wealthy Harris Sinclair, who suffered a traumatic head injury two years prior and is trying to recover her memories from that summer. There are twists and turns as we look into the life of a rich family and see all the secrets that lie in the shadows of what it takes to uphold the image of a happy family. "We were liars," while somewhat confusing at first, makes for an interesting read.

The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes

"The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes", a Hunger Games prequel by Suzanne Collins, follows the story of a young President Snow. Coriolanus Snow, a student of the Capitol Academy, is one 24 chosen to mentor a tribute in the 10th Hunger Games and desires victory to pay for university and restore prestige to his family name. Collins' writing makes you empathize with the young Snow and his struggles of young love, heartbreak, grief, and even desire for power. Do not hesitate to open this book up and dive in, because you will not regret it.

The Maze Runner

A dystopian novel by James Dashner, "The Maze Runner" takes place in a world with an apocalyptic illness called the Flare. It is written from the perspective of Thomas, a 16 year old boy who wakes up one day with his memories gone in a artificially produced maze. The maze is a test from an organization called WICKED, who are running experiments on children to find a cure for the Flare. Thomas, along with the rest of the Gladers, has to find a way to escape before they all die. "The Maze Runner" is a well-written story, exploring the human nature of trial-and-error that you will thoroughly enjoy if you decide to pick it up.

The Naturals

Written by Jennifer Lynn Barnes, "The Naturals" follows a girl named Cassie, who is recruited by the FBI to join a special program. Cassie, along with the other naturals, Dean, Lia, Michael, and Sloane, are originally restricted to cold cases, but when one active case starts to hit a little too close to home for Cassie, the group dives in. "The Naturals" explores the ideas of found family, grief, gifted abilities, and moral dilemmas. If you enjoy true crime and murder mysteries, you are sure to enjoy this book.
